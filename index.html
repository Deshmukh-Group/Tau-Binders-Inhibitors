In plain terms: Tau is a brain protein that normally stabilises the "tracks" (microtubules) inside nerve cells. Heparin / heparan-sulfate (HS) is a long, heavily negatively-charged sugar chain. When HS sticks to tau it makes tau misfold and clump into the tangles seen in Alzheimer's disease — so knowing where on tau HS grabs hold matters. Tau is a floppy, string-like molecule (no fixed 3-D shape) built from the regions below. HS binds wherever tau is positively charged (the proline-rich and "repeat" middle); it is pushed away from the negatively-charged front end.

02Why this matters — how HS drives tau aggregation

A plain-language primer on the disease mechanism behind this study, with the key papers.

First, a common mix-up: tangles vs plaques Alzheimer's has two different protein deposits. Amyloid-β forms the plaques — clumps outside the neurons. Tau is different: it forms neurofibrillary tangles inside the neuron (made of paired helical filaments). So tau makes intracellular tangles, not plaques — both are hallmarks of the disease, but they are distinct proteins in distinct places.
How HS catalyses tau aggregation: (1) soluble floppy tau hides its sticky motifs; (2) the negatively-charged HS binds tau's positively-charged repeats, neutralising charge and opening it up so the sticky PHF6/PHF6* motifs are exposed; (3) many tau molecules line up and their motifs interlock; (4) the motifs zip into a cross-β fibril.

What HS does

Tau is normally floppy and positively charged. HS/heparin is a long, heavily negatively-charged sugar; opposite charges stick, so HS neutralises tau's repulsive positive charge and acts as a cofactor scaffold that glues many tau molecules together to nucleate fibrils (Goedert 1996; Kampers 1996; the cofactor is even a structural part of the fibril — Fichou 2018). The same cell-surface sugars (HSPGs) are also the doorway that lets misfolded tau seeds enter neighbouring cells and spread the disease (Holmes 2013; Stopschinski 2018) — so blocking the tau–HS contact can reduce uptake and seeding (a therapeutic angle, and why mapping these residues matters).

Single tau, or many? → many

The β-sheet fibril is built by many tau molecules together, not one folding alone. A single tau is intrinsically disordered and even folds into a loose "paperclip" that hides its sticky segments (Jeganathan 2006). The process is two-step: first an individual monomer changes shape into a "seed-competent" state that exposes the hexapeptides PHF6 (³⁰⁶VQIVYK³¹¹) and PHF6* (²⁷⁵VQIINK²⁸⁰) (Mirbaha 2018); then strands from different tau molecules stack into an intermolecular "steric-zipper" cross-β spine (von Bergen 2000; Sawaya 2007). So the β-sheet is intermolecular.

Why our binding map is mechanistically relevant HS binds tau's basic R2/R3 repeat region — exactly where the aggregation-seeding motifs PHF6* (R2) and PHF6 (R3) live and where the fibril core forms (Sibille 2006; von Bergen 2000; Zhang 2019). HS binding and fibril nucleation are therefore spatially coupled: the sugar latches onto the very region that becomes the fibril spine — and our top binding residues (K281, K290, K294, K311, H330…) sit right in it.
Caveat Heparin-induced tau filaments are a widely-used in vitro model of tau assembly; their cryo-EM structures are polymorphic and differ from genuine Alzheimer's/Pick's-disease filaments (Zhang 2019). Read this as the mechanism of HS-driven assembly, not an exact replica of disease tangles.

Why favorability varies — regionally

Local sequence charge is a weak predictor (r = -0.148): it is not simply "a lysine next to other lysines." The effect is regional — HS (a polyanion) is attracted to the basic proline-rich/repeat block and repelled from the acidic N-terminal projection domain. 16Fast merged-shell aggregation — setup

A complementary, much longer aggregation module. Instead of packing all solutes into one large box and solvating the whole cell (~0.9 M atoms), every solute carries its own thin 8 Å conformal water shell — so the same wall-clock delivers far more sampling: the merged-shell box runs at ≈39 ns/day (no HS) / ≈34 ns/day (15 HS) on one A30 GPU, versus ≈6 ns/day for the bulk-solvated box on 16 CPU cores (≈6× the throughput). Two systems isolate the role of heparan sulfate: 30 tau alone vs 30 tau + 15 HS.

Why a second protocol The bulk-solvated NPT box (§11–15) is the thermodynamic reference; this build is the kinetic / visualization companion. Pre-hydrating each fragment as a "blob" cuts the water ~10× (≈5× fewer atoms) and starts the solutes proximal; that smaller system on the A30 GPU is what lifts throughput to ≈34–39 ns/day, so aggregation is reached in a few ns and we can then watch the condensate mature for 100+ ns — long enough to catch discrete cluster-fusion events.

How the merged-shell build works

Built in two stages by build_merged.py. (1) Blobs — each unique solute (the five ~88-residue tau segments tiling residues 1–441, plus one HS octasaccharide) is solvated, then trimmed to a conformal 8 Å water shell (waters within 2.6 Å of solute dropped). (2) Merge — blob copies are placed on a cubic 4³ grid in random orientations, spaced by the blob diameter (~70 Å) + 3 Å so they cannot clash but their shells touch; assembled into one CHARMM PSF with per-fragment segids (S11…S56, HS1…HS15); then neutralised and brought to 150 mM NaCl by swapping far-from-solute waters for ions. The cell is pinned to 284.8 Å for both systems so they are directly comparable.

Production (OpenMM CUDA)

CHARMM36m protein + CHARMM36 carb/heparin force field; PME, 1.2 nm cutoff, 1.0 nm switch, H-bonds constrained, rigid water; Langevin-middle integrator, 310 K, 2 fs. Minimise → 200 ps NVT equilibration → NVT production, frames every 10 ps. The no-HS box ran 100 ns in one shot; the 15-HS box ran 20 ns then a true restart (positions + velocities + box) for 100 ns more = 120 ns. A30 throughput ≈ 34–39 ns/day.

Scope & limitations — read before interpreting This is a fast screening / visualization proxy, not a quantitative free-energy method. The blobs start with a water–vacuum interface, so initial coalescence is driven partly by water surface tension (shells minimise their free surface), which conflates cohesion with specific affinity; the ensemble is NVT in a fixed cell with initial vacuum gaps, not constant-pressure bulk. The contact-/bridging-type conclusions are robust; absolute rates are not. For aggregation thermodynamics the bulk-solvated NPT box remains the reference.
